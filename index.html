My Story

Every Journey Leaves a Story

My journey began in Pokhara, Nepal, where the mountains first taught me to look beyond the horizon. Even as a child, I was curious about places I had never seen, people I had never met, and cultures I had only imagined. I often wondered what life looked like beyond my own world.

That curiosity eventually carried me to Japan, where I spent nearly six years studying, working, travelling, and building a new life. What I expected to be a place for education became a place of transformation. I learned a new language, adapted to a different culture, built friendships with people from many countries, and discovered that growth often begins outside our comfort zone.

Some of my most meaningful memories were not extraordinary moments, but ordinary ones: walking home beneath cherry blossoms, sharing meals with friends after class, hearing Japanese customers encourage me with "Nihongo sugoi desu", watching the lights of the Nagoya skyline from my apartment balcony after a long day, and realizing how far I had travelled—not only across countries, but as a person.

Wild Spirit is my way of preserving those moments. It is not simply a travel website or a portfolio. It is a living memoir of curiosity, resilience, friendship, and discovery—a place where every photograph, every story, and every journey reflects a chapter of my life, while reminding me that the world still holds many places waiting to be explored.

"The world became my classroom. Japan became my second home. Curiosity became my compass."

1997 – 2017

🇳🇵 Nepal — Where It Began

Growing up in Pokhara, Nepal, surrounded by mountains, lakes, and nature, I developed a deep curiosity about the world beyond the horizon. Those early years planted the dream of travelling, learning new cultures, and discovering stories waiting in distant places.

2017 – 2021

🇯🇵 Japan — A Chapter That Changed My Life

Japan became much more than a place to study. During nearly six unforgettable years, I learned a new language, embraced a different culture, built friendships with people from many countries, and discovered how stepping outside my comfort zone transformed not only my skills, but my perspective on life itself.
